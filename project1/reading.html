Storytime

Sometimes, the best way to recharge is to retreat into the safe haven of a good book. Here, we’ve gathered not loud bestsellers, but quiet, healing stories. Choose a theme that resonates with your soul, open a section, and let the words wrap you in comfort.

"The House in the Cerulean Sea" — TJ Klune

Linus Baker is a law-abiding and fastidious inspector for the Department in Charge of Magical Youth. His life is strictly governed by rules and regulations. Everything changes when he receives a top-secret assignment from High Command: to travel to a remote island and spend a month at the Marsyas Orphanage to assess the danger posed by the six unusual children living there and determine whether the facility should be shut down.

"A Psalm for the Wild-Built" — Becky Chambers

Centuries after the robots on the planet Panga gained self-awareness, abandoned their factories, and peacefully retreated into the wild forests, they have become figures of myth. Dex, a city dweller, decides to change careers and becomes a traveling tea monk, offering people conversation and herbal infusions. Seeking solitude, Dex ventures deep into the uncharted wilderness, where they unexpectedly encounter a rogue robot that has emerged from the wilds to discover what modern humans truly need.

"The Boy, the Mole, the Fox and the Horse" — Charlie Mackesy

A small, lost boy travels across a snowy, wild landscape in search of his home. Along the way, he encounters three different companions one by one: a voracious mole, a wary fox, and a wise horse. Together, they overcome obstacles, explore unfamiliar terrain, and converse about fear, loneliness, acceptance, and the challenges of their journey.

"The Travelling Cat Chronicles" — Hiro Arikawa

The story is told from the perspective of a stray cat named Nana, who was rescued and taken in by a young man named Satoru. After several years of happy life together, Satoru is forced—due to personal circumstances he keeps hidden from the cat—to find a new owner for Nana. Together, they embark on a journey across Japan in a silver van to visit Satoru’s old school and university friends and see if any of them might be able to take the cat in.

"Before the Coffee Gets Cold" — Toshikazu Kawaguchi

Tucked away in a quiet Tokyo alley is a small basement café where, according to urban legend, patrons are offered the chance to travel through time. However, doing so requires adhering to a strict set of rules: the journey can only take place within that specific room, the present cannot be altered, and—most importantly—one must return before the poured cup of coffee goes cold. The story follows four different patrons who decide to take this chance.
